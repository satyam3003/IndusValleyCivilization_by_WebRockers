Indus Valley civilization

About Indus Valley Civilization

Indus Valley Civilization was discovered in the Southern Asia in early 20th century. This is a Bronze Age Civilization dating back to 3000 BCE to 1000 BCE. This civilization was located at the basins of Indus River and is widespread starting from Northeast Afghanistan, Pakistan till the north western India.

Architecture of Indus Valley

The cities of this civilization consists of well laid-out streets and fine houses constructed with baked bricks. The most surprising and impressing thing of these cities are their planned drainage and water supply systems. The great baths, city walls, granaries, warehouses, dockyards also shows the advanced architecture of the Indus valley Civilization.

Lifestyle in Indus Valley Civilization

Among all the civilizations that are known, Indus Valley Civilization appears to have developed and thrived without warfare or violent competitions. There are no palaces to be discovered and very few structures can be identified as having had a religious function. Agriculture and Animal Husbandry along with trading seems to be the major occupation of this civilization.

Mohenjo daro

Mohenjo-daro is one of the largest settlements found in Sindh region of Pakisthan. Its excavation began in 1922 and the grid planned city along with various artifact seals were discovered. The Great-Bath is one of the major buildings located at Mohenjo-daro

Harappa Civilization

Harappa is a 150 acres archaeological site located in the Punjab region of Pakisthan. This city is known for its fortified wall around the city, the clay brick houses and the artifacts and the seals with an unknown script which suggests that the city dated back to 2600 BC to 1900 BCE.

The Priest-King

Priest King statue was discovered at the Mohejo-daro archaeological site and is marked to be dated from 2200BCE to 1900 BCE. This statue carved of a stone is decorated with a robe on the left shoulder with sacred symbols along with well carved jewellery on the hand and fore-head. This statue considered as a priest may be an evidence that the civilization was run by priests.

GREAT BATH

The Great Bath is a public building structure that was discovered at Mohenjo-daro which might belong to 3000 BCE. This structure is known as the " Earliest Watertank of the ancient world." The tank of 11.88m x 7.01m with a depth of 2.43m approx. is said to be waterproofed with help of finely fitted bricks, mud plaster and bitumen that was used in the tank.
