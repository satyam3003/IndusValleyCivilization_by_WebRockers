Culture, History, Monuments, Artifacts of Indus Valley Civilization...

Motif in the ancient Indus Valley.

The word Motif means "A repeated Structure of Drawing.". Since the excavation are numerous UNICORN SEALS found in the region. They Consist a Unicorn in common and along with it a series of symbols which Scientist are still unable to understand. These structures are said to be of 3300 BCE to 1600 BCE. Every Sculpture is different from other in terms of size, structure, date, writing.
Large unicorn seal (H99-4064/8796-01) found on the floor of Room 591 in Trench 43, dating to late Period 3C. This is one of the largest seals found from any Indus site.

Were Coins Used in Indus Valley Civilization?

The answer to this question is unknown. As Indus valley civilization dates back to 3600 BCE to 1700 BCE, we can not really predict if the excavations found are of coins or something else.
But, there are excavations of several coin like metal structure made up of silver and bronze. These seals have extensive carvings and are very beautiful. The remains Can be found in Museum of Delhi and in Pakistan.
The Seals were made by chiseling then into beautiful carving. and this process took a long time to complete.

The Dancing Girl

Between 2300 BCE to 1750 BCE the Dancing Girl sculpture was made out of bronze with the help of wax-casting. This happened in Mohenjo-Daro in current day pakistan. the statue is 10.5 centimeters tall that is around 4.1 inch.
The statue shows a naked lady standing in stylish fashion and confidence. The girl is wearing a lot of bangles and ornaments in her neck. this statue was discovered by Ernest Mackay in 1926 in Hr Area of Mohanjo-Daro. This is one of twe statues consisting similar structure.

The Idols and Artifacts

Many Idols were discovered from Indus Valley at the time of excavation. The idols were: A group of terracotta figurines from Harappa, Ox- or water buffalo-drawn cart, Female figures, Head of a female figurine with a "tiara" headdress, Three male figures, Three Early Harappan zebu figurines and a lot more.
These idols help us understand the culture and the technology the people used in that period. The idols are very rare and very fascinating.

Ancient Indus Valley civilization cooking

The rooms of the upper town were obviously built for upper classes. They had private pathed brick baths and a remarkable network of drains and cesspools. Cooking pots during the Ravi Phase were made in large globular shapes that had a low center of gravity to keep them from tipping over when filled with food. In order to protect the fine clay from cracking due to the heat of the fire, the exterior was covered with a slurry of coarse sandy clay mixed with calcium carbonate nodules and some pebbles. A small black fired bowl is seen in the foreground. Period III, Harappan, 2300-2200 B. C.
